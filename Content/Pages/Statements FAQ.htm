Statements FAQ

Q: How do I suppress statements?

A: Statements can be suppressed at the Account level or Visit level based upon the settings in Statement Option.

Statements can be also be suppressed on an individual account or visit by several methods:

  • To suppress an individual account from creating statements, select the Statement Suppress check box on the Statement Information tab of the Patient Account.
  • To suppress visits that have been assigned to collections from appearing on statements, select the Suppress Statement check box for the individual visits on the Collection Tab.
  • To suppress visits that have been assigned a Visit Category, select the category on the Visit Ledger tab of the patient account and make sure that that category is also set up for statement suppression in Statement Option.

 

Q: What is the Statement Frequency:

A: All statements include "at least once a month" logic. This means that, regardless of activity, inactivity or cycle settings, every account with self-pay balances or Patient Responsibility Amounts will have a statement generated at least once every 30 days by default.

 

Q: What are the available options for Statement Generation?

Statement Trigger 1: Due From Insurance

  • Statements will be generated for all accounts regardless of whether the balance is due from the insurance or set to self pay.
    • To prevent statements from being created for specific payers, such as Medicaid Plans or Work Comp carriers, select the Suppress Statements check box on the Other Configurations tab in Administration > Payer > Configuration.

Statement Trigger 2: Due From Self Pay

  • Statements will be created once a visit balance has been transferred to self-pay status.

Statement Trigger 3: Immediately bill patient responsibility amount

  • In the Patient Account >Visit Ledger tab, there is a column for Patient Resp Amount. This column can also be found in Billing > Charge Entry and Billing > Payments and Adjustments on the Line Item Posting tab. If this check box is checked in the Statement Option form, then any amount entered in this column will trigger a statement, regardless of the fact that the remainder of the balance may still be due from the insurance.

Q. What will trigger a statement?

A. Transactions

Any Activity Message for which the Trigger? check box has been checked in the Statement Option Triggers tab will initiate the creation of a statement if the Statement Triggers (see above) warrant a statement and no visit or account suppression criteria are present.

  • Statements will continue to be created on a monthly bases until paid in full. For monthly statements, the Reason will simply appear as Inactivity.

 

Q. What Transactions Appear on Statements?

A. Centers have three choices available for Statement Type:

  1. Show Balance Only: No individual transactions will appear on a statement; only a ‘Balance Due’.
  2. Show New Tx Only:Transactions will only appear on a statement one time; old transactions on subsequent statements will be rolled up into a Balance Forward line.
  3. Show All Tx: All transactions from non-suppressed visits will appear on every statement.

 

Q. What Statement Messages will appear on our statements?

A. Specific messages should be set up in Administration > Configuration > Statement Message to print on statements based on the Transactions and Inactivity triggers. A statement can only have one message. In the normal statement generation process, the message tied to the most recent trigger will print on the statement.

  • When printing statements, the message that will print is based on the Statement Option and Triggers, but users may override by selecting a different message from the drop down in the Statement Message column or by typing a custom message into that field for the visit selected.
